Bay Area series, 2021
24” x 24”

Each painting in the Bay Area series is an exercise in imagining what an iconic sight could look like in a dystopian future, where Earth has been through an environmental disaster and all the oceans & most of the atmosphere have disappeared.
The Palace of Fine Arts painting depicts a desert in place of the San Francisco bay and the Fine Arts Palace ruined building.
The Alcatraz Island features a canyon between the Alcatraz Island and the city of San Francisco, where the ocean floor used to be.
Both paintings were inspired by science-fiction literature, especially the Dune series by Frank Herbert and NASA Perseverance rover landing on Mars.

NASA Insight Platform
2015 - 2020
Web design & development
HTML, JavaScript (Backbone, Node, Koa, Express), CSS
NASA Insight is a web platform that integrates, aggregates and analyzes data from various technical and managerial data sources within NASA.
Used by several NASA teams including Extravehicular Activity (EVA) office at Johnson Space Center, office of Safety and Mission Assurance at Stennis Space Center, and Information Resource Directorate at Johnson Space Center, among others.
Part of a 3-5 person engineering team, responsible for design & development of search & view pages, visual style guides, and micro-services that post-process data.
I’m an artist & designer.
I use both traditional media & digital media.
In my studio art practice, I use acrylic paints, charcoal & graphite.
In my digital art practice, I design user interfaces, icons & branding styles for websites using modern, clean and minimalist aesthetics.
My studio art draws themes from literary works of science-fiction, fantasy and philosophy. I aim to capture the same feeling you get when you are immersed in a great book, losing all sense of the world around you.
I relish the opportunity to create a coherent visual narrative both in my user interface design and studio art.
